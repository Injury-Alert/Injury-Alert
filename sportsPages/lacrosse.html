Hip Injury Prevention

The most common injuries in Lacrosse occur at the Head, Wrist, Hip, Knee, and Back. Common injuries for Lacrosse include: Ankle sprain, Head and Face Contusion, Concussion, Knee Sprain, Wrist Fracture, Hip Flexor Sprain, and Lower Back Pain.

Sets and Reps Breakdown

Hip Injuries:


For Hip injuries we strongly recommend to strengthen the muscles and tendons around the hips through deep range of motion

Floor bridge: Lie on the floor on your back with your knees bent and feet flat. Squeeze your glutes and push your hips off the ground while pushing down the heels. Drop the hips and repeat. Complete one set of 20 reps.

Standing Hip Abduction: using something to help balance, stand on one leg. Bring the other leg across the body and hold then swing it out to the side for 5 seconds. Complete one set of 10-15 reps per leg.

Concussions:


Neck Curls: Lay on your back and perform slow and controlled neck curls, touching the chin to the chest, 1-2 times a week 3 sets of 25 reps
