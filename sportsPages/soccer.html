Soccer Injury Prevention

The most common injuries in Soccer occur at the lower body. Common injuries for Soccer include: ACL tears, Meniscus Tears, Adductor Strains, and ankle sprains.

Sets and Reps Breakdown

ACL Tear:


For any related knee tear, we strongly reccomend to strenthen the muscles and tendons around the knee through deep range of motion
Backwards Sled: Go until you feel a burn in the quadtricep muscles located above knee and work through it, 1-2 Times a week
VMO Squat: Elevate the heels on a surface and perform squats so that the knee can travel over the toes, 2-3 sets of 12-15 Reps progressively increasing the weight as you get more comfortable.
Tibia Curls: Stand with your back against the wall with feet off the wall. Pull the toes torward the knee feeling the shin muscle contract
(Progression): Place foot in the grip of a kettle bell and perform weighted curls with the leg supported by a bench

Adductor Strain:


Isometric Holds: Lay on your back with you knees bent. Place an object between your knees, squeeze the object and perform a hip thrust holding for 30 seconds for 3 sets.
(Progression): Grab a bench or chair, get on your side and place top leg on object and let other leg go beneath. Perform reps squeezing your addductos together, 3 sets of 12-15
